Submission Guidelines

1. Scope üéØ

We welcome submissions covering the following areas:

  • Foundations & Model Architectures: Core theoretical underpinnings and structural designs for generative AI, including machine learning paradigms, deep learning architectures, statistical learning theory, and probabilistic modeling approaches.
  • Optimization & System Efficiency: Algorithmic optimization strategies and system-level solutions to enhance generative AI training and deployment, covering convex/non-convex optimization, distributed computing, hardware adaptation, and generative reinforcement learning.
  • Trustworthiness & Evaluation: Frameworks for ensuring reliability, safety, and validity of generative AI systems, including fairness, robustness, privacy, interpretability, and rigorous evaluation methodologies.
  • Application-Driven Innovation: Innovative generative AI techniques, datasets, and problem-solving approaches tailored to real-world domains such as healthcare, biosciences, sustainability, and climate science.

2. Manuscript Requirements üìù

  • All manuscripts must be written in English with clear and concise language.
  • Manuscripts should be formatted in A4 size, double-spaced, with 12-point Arial font.
  • Maximum length: 15 pages (including figures and references).
  • Include a title page with authors' names, affiliations, and contact information.

3. Submission Process üì§

  1. Submit your manuscript via our online submission system (link coming soon).
  2. Include a cover letter stating the novelty and significance of your work.
  3. All submissions are subject to double-blind peer review.
  4. Authors will receive review comments within 3 weeks of submission.

4. Manuscript Recommendations üí°

Every manuscript must:

  • State clearly about the problem to be solved and what the contribution of the work is to the research community relevant to the transactions;
  • State clearly why this contribution is novel;
  • Cite the published literature most closely related to the manuscript; and state what is distinctive and new about the current manuscript relative to these previously published works.

5. AIGC Usage Policy ü§ñ

Authors are permitted to use AI-generated content (AIGC) tools in the preparation of their manuscripts, provided that:

  • All uses of AIGC tools must be explicitly declared in the manuscript.
  • Authors must specify which parts of the manuscript were generated or assisted by AI tools.
  • Authors remain fully responsible for the accuracy, integrity, and originality of the content.
  • Any AI-generated content must be properly reviewed, edited, and verified by the authors.

Failure to declare the use of AI tools may result in rejection of the manuscript.

6. Copyright and Ethics üìÑ

Authors must confirm that the manuscript is original and has not been published elsewhere. By submitting, authors agree to the journal's copyright policy and ethical guidelines.

7. Publication Fees üí∞

Publication Fees: This publication is supported by subscriptions and applicable Article Processing Charges (APCs). Although there is no cost for publishing with TGAI, authors may wish to take advantage of some of our fee-based offerings.
