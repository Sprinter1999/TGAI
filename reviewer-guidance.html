Reviewer Guidance

1. Review Timeline

Reviewers are expected to complete their reviews within 2 weeks of accepting the review request. If you require additional time, please contact the editorial office as soon as possible.

2. Ethical Guidelines

  • No AI Usage: Reviewers are not permitted to directly use AI tools to write the final review. It is acceptable to use AI tools to help reviewers understand the manuscript, but all reviews must be based on your own expertise and critical analysis.
  • Maintain confidentiality of the manuscript and review process.
  • Decline to review if you have a conflict of interest with any author.
  • Provide objective, fair, and constructive feedback.

3. Review Content

Your review should include:

3.1 Strengths

  • Identify the key contributions and innovations of the manuscript.
  • Highlight well-executed aspects of the research and writing.
  • Acknowledge the potential impact of the work on the field.

3.2 Weaknesses and Suggestions for Improvement

  • Identify any gaps in the research design or methodology.
  • Note any issues with the experimental setup or results interpretation.
  • Point out areas where the writing could be improved for clarity.
  • Address any concerns about the novelty or significance of the work.

4. Review Format

  • Start with an overall assessment of the manuscript.
  • Organize your feedback into clear sections (strengths, weaknesses, suggestions for improvement).
  • Be specific and provide examples to support your comments.
  • Conclude with a recommendation (accept, minor revision, major revision, reject) and justify your decision.

5. Review Criteria

  • Novelty: Is the work original and does it contribute new knowledge to the field?
  • Significance: Does the work address an important problem in generative AI?
  • Methodology: Is the research approach sound and well-executed?
  • Results: Are the results valid, reliable, and properly interpreted?
  • Writing: Is the manuscript well-written, clear, and logically organized?
  • References: Is the literature review comprehensive and up-to-date?

6. Confidentiality

All manuscripts and review materials are confidential. Do not share the manuscript or your review with anyone else without prior authorization from the editorial office.

7. Contact Information

If you have any questions or concerns about the review process, please contact the editorial office at: editorial@tgai-journal.org
